Fight for an Open Internet!

Today, when we pay for an internet plan, we get access to the entire internet — whether it is popular apps and sites like WhatsApp, Facebook and YouTube, or the millions of less popular ones. The principle that Internet Service Providers (ISPs) should provide equal access to all websites is known as net neutrality.

We might begin to lose this freedom by the end of April. Due to intense lobbying by telecom operators, the Indian Government is considering new rules that would allow operators like Reliance, Vodafone and Airtel to block apps and websites at will. Their aim is to block WhatsApp to force expensive SMS packs on us, to charge us exorbitantly for YouTube, or to use the threat of blocking to extort money from startups — a cost we’ll bear ultimately as free apps become paid or go out of business.

If net neutrality ends, it would be the end of the free, innovative internet that we know today. Popular free services like Facebook would never exist if they had to pay money to every ISP in the world before they could start.

If you care about the future of the open internet in India, please join us to oppose this proposal. Together, let’s remind the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) that their job is to protect the rights of consumers, not the profit margins of telcos. Let's tell them that when we pay for an internet plan, we expect access to the entire internet. Not just the apps that Airtel and Vodafone approve of.
